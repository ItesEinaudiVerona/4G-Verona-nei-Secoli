JULIET'S HOUSE

History/b>

Castelvecchio is one of the most important monuments of the city. Originally called the castle of San Martino in Aquaro, it was built by Cangrande della Scala between 1354 and 1357, as a defensive castle and noble residence. The castle and the bridge were built to protect the Scala family from Venice, Mantua and Milan. Infact, the bridge faces north, this way the rest of Della Scala family managed to escape to Bavaria about 1380 After the fall of the Scala family it was used as an arms depot by the Venetians and in the 18th century it housed the Military Academy of the Serenissima; In the period of Napoleonic rule (1797-1814) and Austrian rule (1797-1866) it was traumatic for Verona. With the peace of Luneville (1801), the city was divided in two: the French ruled on the right side of the Adige, while the opposite side was under the control of the Austrians. Once the tops of the towers and the crenellated walkways of the castle were dismantled, a fort was built by the French to defend themselves against the Austrians, whose headquarters substantially correspond to the current wing of the Gallery. Between 1923 and 1926 a radical restoration was started which dismantled the military characteristics of the monument, with the insertion of reused late Gothic and Renaissance architectural elements and the restoration of the battlements and the tower covers (eliminated in the Napoleonic era). In 1928 it became a museum, which exhibits important collections of medieval, Renaissance and modern art.

Description

The castle is one of the most popular monuments of Verona. Inside it's divided into three different parts: Piazza D'Armi, corte della Reggia or residential part and the Corte del Mastio, which divides the two previous courts. In Piazza D'Armi was the biggest part where the solidiers trained for combat. And today in this place you can find the famous museo of Castelvecchio. Corte della Reggia was the place that the Cangrande's family lived. The ponte scaligero supported by three arcades, it was rebuilt in 1945 after war ruin and today again it is consider one of the beautiful monuments of the castle. The castle has seven towers.

History of Romeo and Juliet

An ancient revenge between two powerful families, Montecchi and Capuleti, breaks out in a fraudulent quarrel, finding Juliet and Romeo dead at the end of the story. At a Capuleti evening, masked Montecchi enter, including Romeo. This evening he meets and falls in love with Juliet, but the two will never be able to love each other due to the disagreements between the two families. The two plan to run away so they can get married. The story tells of the conflicted love between Romeo Montecchi and Juliet Capuleti, belonging to two rival families. Romeo and Juliet get married in secret. In a fight Romeo kills a man to avenge the death of Mercuzio, a friend of his, and is exiled for this. Juliet, in order not to marry Paride, a young nobleman related to the prince, drinks a potion that makes her appear dead. Romeo rushes to Juliet's tomb believing her to be dead and kills himself. When Juliet wakes up, seeing Romeo dead, she kills herself.

  • Giulietta e Romeo
    Il mito di Giulietta e Romeo viene celebrato in spettacoli teatrali e rivisitazioni durante tutto l’anno. La rassegna più famosa è il Festival shakespeariano che si svolge ogni estate al Teatro Romano, secondo in Europa dopo quello di Stratford on Avon. Tra le iniziative di accoglienza turistica maggiormente apprezzate dai visitatori Benvenuti a Verona. Romeo e Giulietta una storia d’amore: le più belle scene del dramma shakespeariano vengono recitate durante il periodo estivo, in italiano e in inglese, nella loro cornice ideale, il cortile e il balcone di Giulietta, dove la leggenda vuole che i due innamorati si siano realmente conosciuti ed amati.
  • Presepi e tradizioni
    Per tutta la durata delle feste natalizie si tiene la Rassegna Internazionale dei Presepi nell’Arte e nella Tradizione, allestita negli arcovoli dell’Arena. La mostra ospita ogni anno circa 400 esemplari provenienti dai più importanti musei internazionali e da collezioni private. L’allestimento è realizzato con suggestivi effetti speciali ed offre un singolare spettacolo, che richiama ogni anno decine di migliaia di visitatori. Tocatì, Festival Internazionale dei giochi di strada Nel periodo natalizio piazza Bra ospita anche la Stella cometa, una grande archi-scultura in metallo dell’architetto Rinaldo Olivieri, che spunta dal centro dell’Arena disegnando uno spettacolare arco luminoso alto settanta metri. Ideata per l’inaugurazione della prima Rassegna internazionale dei Presepi, la Stella cometa è divenuta il simbolo più famoso del Natale veronese. E’ entrato a pieno titolo nelle tradizioni veronesi anche il Tocatì, festival internazionale dei giochi di strada, che si tiene a fine settembre nel centro storico della città: per tre giorni spariscono le auto per lasciare posto a decine di giochi tradizionali, che coinvolgono grandi e piccini. Ogni anno il Tocatì accoglie una Nazione ospite d’onore, che presenta la propria cultura e le proprie tradizioni attraverso spettacolari esibizioni.
